Executive Summary

This report presents comprehensive spatial isoform analysis results from SPLISOSM on glioma data using both Visium ONT (11 samples) and Visium SR (13 samples), with validation using an independent scRNA-seq dataset (32,304 cells, 16 cell states) and SpaCET spot deconvolution. All three HSIC test statistics (IR=Isoform Ratio, GC=Gene Counts, IC=Isoform Counts) were computed for 32 SecAct target genes across 24 total samples. Seven genes achieved TIER1_FULL validation (significant in both spatial and scRNA-seq), including VCAN with 99% ΔPSI between macrophages and tumor cells. SpaCET deconvolution confirms that VCAN isoform ratios correlate positively with tumor content (r=+0.19) and negatively with macrophage content (r=-0.17) at the spot level.

10. Methods Summary

Visium ONT Analysis (Discovery)

  • Tool: SPLISOSM (Spatial Isoform Statistical Omnibus Model)
  • Primary Statistic: HSIC-IR (Hilbert-Schmidt Independence Criterion for Isoform Ratio)
  • Kernel: Full-rank ICAR spatial kernel (k=15 neighbors, ρ=0.99)
  • P-value: Liu's chi-squared moment matching
  • Targets: 26 SecAct genes with 2+ isoforms detected
  • Significance threshold: HSIC-IR p-value < 0.05

scRNA-seq Analysis (Validation)

  • Tool: MARVEL (single-cell splicing)
  • Metric: PSI (Percent Spliced In)
  • Events: SE, MXE, A5SS, A3SS, AFE, ALE
  • Statistical test: Wilcoxon rank-sum between tumor states

SpaCET Deconvolution (Cell Type Validation)

  • Tool: SpaCET v1.0 (Spatial Cellular Estimator for Tumors)
  • Cancer type: GBM signature
  • Cell types: 14 major lineage types
  • Correlation: Spearman's rho between cell proportion and isoform fraction
  • Samples: 13 Visium SR glioma samples

13. Conclusions

Key Findings Summary

  1. 7 genes achieved TIER1_FULL validation (SPP1, CLU, HMGB1, CD74, APP, SPARCL1, VCAN) with both significant spatial HSIC-IR patterns and scRNA-seq differential splicing.
  2. VCAN shows the largest effect with 99% ΔPSI between macrophages (low) and tumor cells (high), confirmed by SpaCET deconvolution showing positive tumor correlation (r=+0.19) and negative macrophage correlation (r=-0.17).
  3. HSIC-GC (expression) is near-universal while HSIC-IR (isoform ratio) is selective, demonstrating that isoform switching occurs in a subset of spatially variable genes.
  4. 19 TIER2 genes show spatial-only patterns without scRNA validation, suggesting microenvironment-driven APA regulation independent of cell identity.
  5. Platform complementarity: SR detects 3' UTR/APA variation while ONT captures full-length splice isoforms - both are biologically relevant.

Biological Implications

The spatial isoform patterns identified by SPLISOSM reveal two distinct regulatory mechanisms:

  • Cell-intrinsic splicing (TIER1): Genes like VCAN and APP have cell-type-specific splicing programs that create spatial patterns reflecting cellular composition of the tumor microenvironment.
  • Microenvironment-driven regulation (TIER2): Genes like B2M, MIF, and SPARC show spatial patterns without cell-type specificity, suggesting that local signals (hypoxia, ECM, cytokines) regulate isoform usage independently of cell identity.

These findings have implications for understanding how the tumor microenvironment modulates secreted protein function through post-transcriptional mechanisms.
